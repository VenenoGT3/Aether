# Aether Creator Program Terms — DRAFT

> **STATUS: ENGINEERING DRAFT — NOT IN FORCE.**
> This document defines the operative rules the product already enforces (or
> is built to enforce) so legal counsel can turn them into binding terms. It
> is written for an EU-established platform (Italian/EU law assumed — counsel
> to confirm governing law and consumer/commercial splits). Nothing here is
> legal advice. Do not publish without counsel review.

The structure deliberately mirrors what Content Rewards' US terms cover, plus
the EU-specific obligations a US platform does not carry (DAC7, GDPR, UCPD /
AGCM disclosure rules, platform fairness).

## 1. Definitions

- **Platform** — Aether, operated by [ENTITY, registered office, VAT no.].
- **Brand** — a business account funding a campaign budget pool.
- **Creator** — an influencer account submitting content to campaigns.
- **Verified Views** — view counts retrieved by the Platform directly from
  the official API of the social network on which the content is published,
  for content whose ownership the Creator has proven via account linking.
- **Legitimate Views** — Verified Views excluding views generated by bots,
  scripts, macros, automated means, paid view-inflation services, incentive
  schemes (prizes, barter, payment for watching), or any other manipulation.
  The Platform's fraud-scoring system flags suspected illegitimate views;
  determinations are made on documented signals (velocity, uniformity,
  engagement-ratio, cross-campaign duplication) — see §6.

## 2. Eligibility

- Creators must be at least 18 years old and hold full legal capacity.
- Creators must complete identity verification through the Platform's
  payment partner (Stripe Connect KYC) before receiving payouts.
- Creators must provide the seller information required by Council Directive
  (EU) 2021/514 (**DAC7**) — see §9. Payouts are withheld where this
  information is missing after two reminders.
- One account per natural person; accounts are non-transferable.

## 3. Campaign participation

- Joining a campaign is free and does not create an employment,
  agency, or exclusive relationship. Creators act as independent
  professionals.
- Only content **published after joining** a campaign accrues billable
  views; views existing at first verification are recorded as a
  **non-billable baseline** (enforced by the earnings engine).
- Each piece of content may be submitted to one campaign only; duplicate or
  cross-campaign submissions of the same content are rejected automatically.
- Submitted content must come from a linked, public social account whose
  ownership the Creator has verified through the Platform.

## 4. Compensation

- Earnings accrue per 1,000 Legitimate Views at the campaign's reward rate,
  within the campaign's budget pool and any per-creator and per-clip bounds
  displayed at the time of joining (qualification floor and per-clip cap).
- Campaign money terms (rate, pool, caps, floors, fees) are **immutable after
  funding** — enforced at the database level. Creators can rely on the terms
  shown when they joined.
- Earnings become withdrawable after the campaign's holdback window; the
  platform currency is the one displayed at funding (EUR by default).
- No compensation accrues after a campaign's pool is exhausted or its end
  date passes. Accrued, verified earnings remain claimable after campaign end.
- The Platform's fee is charged to the Brand on the funded pool. The reward
  rate shown to Creators is what Creators earn.

## 5. Advertising disclosure (EU)

- Creators MUST clearly and prominently disclose the commercial relationship
  in each submitted piece of content, in the language of the target audience
  (e.g. **#ad**, **#sponsorizzato**, **#pubblicità**, or the platform-native
  paid-partnership label), visible without expanding any "more" fold, per the
  Unfair Commercial Practices Directive 2005/29/EC and national guidance
  (in Italy: AGCM and Digital Chart of IAP).
- The submission flow reminds Creators of this obligation; the Platform may
  reject or retroactively disqualify content lacking disclosure.
- Brands must not instruct Creators to omit disclosure; doing so is a
  material breach.

## 6. Fraud, flags, and forfeiture

- Suspected illegitimate views trigger an automated flag with documented
  signals; flagged clips stop accruing and enter review.
- Confirmed manipulation results in: disqualification of the affected
  content, reversal of unconfirmed earnings attributable to illegitimate
  views, and — for repeat or egregious cases — removal from the campaign or
  Platform.
- **EU fairness note (differs from CR's "sole discretion" model):** Creators
  receive the reason category for any disqualification and may contest it
  through the dispute channel in §10 within 14 days. Verified earnings from
  Legitimate Views accrued before the violation are not forfeited unless the
  violation taints them.

## 7. Intellectual property

- Creators retain ownership of their content.
- On submission, the Creator grants the Brand a worldwide, non-exclusive,
  royalty-free licence to use, reproduce, and display the submitted content
  for the Brand's marketing, for [TERM — counsel: avoid CR's perpetual +
  moral-rights-waiver model; moral rights are unwaivable in much of the EU,
  including Italy], and grants the Platform the licence needed to operate the
  service (display, analytics, fraud review).
- Creators warrant the content is original, does not infringe third-party
  rights, and that required music/asset licences are respected per the
  campaign's asset rules.

## 8. Data protection (GDPR)

- The Platform processes Creator and Brand personal data as described in the
  Privacy Policy; legal bases: contract performance (campaign operation,
  payouts), legal obligation (DAC7, accounting), legitimate interest (fraud
  prevention — documented signals only, with human review on contest).
- Linked-account OAuth grants are used solely for ownership verification and
  official metrics; the Platform does not store social tokens beyond what the
  verification flow requires (YouTube grants are revoked immediately after
  verification).
- Data subject rights are exercised via [CONTACT]; supervisory authority:
  [e.g. Garante per la protezione dei dati personali].

## 9. Tax and DAC7 reporting

- Creators are responsible for their own income tax and (where applicable)
  VAT obligations.
- The Platform is a **Reporting Platform Operator** under DAC7 and will
  collect and verify: full name, primary address, TIN (and VAT number where
  applicable), date of birth, and financial account identifiers; report
  consideration paid per quarter to the competent authority annually (by 31
  January); and provide Creators a copy of reported data.
- Payouts are suspended for Creators who fail to provide DAC7 data after two
  reminders and 60 days, as the directive requires.

## 10. Disputes

- Contested rejections/disqualifications: internal review channel with a
  decision target of 14 days (see §6).
- EU consumer/platform rules on ranking transparency and complaint handling
  (P2B Regulation 2019/1150 applies to business users) — counsel to confirm
  scope for Creators acting professionally.
- Governing law and venue: [counsel — note mandatory consumer protections;
  arbitration clauses with class-action waivers in the CR style are not
  generally enforceable against EU consumers].

## 11. Changes and termination

- The Platform may amend these terms with [30 days'] notice; material changes
  to campaign economics never apply retroactively to funded campaigns (§4).
- Either party may terminate the relationship at any time; accrued, verified
  earnings survive termination except in §6 forfeiture cases.

### Counsel checklist (engineering → legal handoff)

1. Entity, registered office, VAT, contact details.
2. Governing law / venue; consumer-vs-professional split for Creators.
3. IP licence term (avoid unwaivable moral-rights conflicts).
4. P2B Regulation applicability to creator accounts.
5. DAC7 registration and reporting mechanics with the tax authority.
6. VAT treatment of the platform fee (B2B reverse charge across member states).
7. DSA touchpoints (notice-and-action for illegal content on campaign pages).
